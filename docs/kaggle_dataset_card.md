## The Most Comprehensive Marathon des Sables Dataset Ever Assembled

The **INFNIT™ Marathon des Sables Dataset** covers 40 editions of the world's most famous self-supported ultramarathon — 1986 through the 40th edition in 2026.

**24,163 anonymised records. 23,183 finishers. 922 DNF records with exact stage dropout location.**

### What's inside

- **40 years of finisher data** — DUV Statistik (1986–2019) + LiveTrail API (2021–2026)
- **922 DNF records** from 2021–2026 with exact stage-level dropout — the first public dataset to capture *where* athletes withdrew
- **Survival phase transition** — Stage 4 (~91km long stage) accounts for 41% of DNFs in normal conditions
- **Athlete demographics** — nationality, gender, age category, year of birth
- **Performance tiers** — ELITE / MID / BACK by scratch ranking percentile
- **Anonymised** — names, clubs, and bib numbers removed (UK GDPR data minimisation)

### Key findings

- S4 dominant collapse: 41.2% of 2026 DNFs — long stage confirmed as primary attrition point
- S2 pacing failure: Stage 2 DNFs were 32.8% faster than finisher pace — overconsumption signature
- Post-S5 survival: athletes who reach Stage 5 almost always finish
- 2021 anomaly: 44.6% DNF rate — extreme heat October edition (highest in modern MDS history)
- Velocity tiers: ELITE 2.10 m/s | MID 1.57 m/s | BACK 1.11 m/s

### Suggested uses

- DNF prediction and survival modelling
- Longitudinal performance trend analysis (40 years)
- Pacing strategy research
- Extreme environment physiology
- Race preparation benchmarking
